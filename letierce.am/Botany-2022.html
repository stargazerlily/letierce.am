Seed of Rare Wetland Native May Store Well After All!

Sabatia campanulata is extremely rare (S1) and endangered in Massachusetts.

Threats to this species include habitat loss, competition with invasives, predation by herbivores, and inbreeding depression.

learn more

Background

The success of seed banking as a conservation strategy is dependent on the compliant storage behavior of seeds.  Plants unsuitable for conventional methods of seed banking are termed exceptional.  Many Gentianaceae are believed to have short-lived seeds, and previous investigations showed Gentianaceae stored in our seed bank achieved 40-50% germination (Fig. 1).  However, Sabatia campanulata (Fig. 2) failed to germinate, and was presumed to be exceptional.  Germination trials were conducted to investigate desiccation and cold sensitivity as responsible factors.
