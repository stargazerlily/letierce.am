Amelie Mona LeTierce

Budding Botanist & Plant Ecologist

Amelie is a student of Plant & Soil Science and Biology at the University of Massachusetts, Amherst. Her research interests include plant-microbe interactions, seeds and plant reproductive biology, genetic conservation, plant propagation, and biotechnology.   She is a student member of the Botanical Society of America and the American Phytopathological Society.
  
This summer, Amelie is an intern with the New England regional conservation organization, Native Plant Trust, where she specializes in seed-bank operations and management, and participates in wild population surveys and other conservation activities.

Amelie is conducting research on the challenges and potential solutions of gene-banking exceptional plant species which may not store well in a traditional seed bank environment. She is designing a project to develop propagation protocols for native terrestrial orchids which are dependent on fungi for proper development.
